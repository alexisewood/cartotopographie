What this is

cartotoplogies is a layered cartographic essay built from contour rings. The rings are not only elevation; they are a reading device—an interface for a collapse of time and space. Each ring is clickable. Each click opens a fragment: a relay between instrument, archive, and afterlife.

How to read

Start on the map. Click a ring to open the right-hand panel. You can close it, choose another ring, or use Prev / Next to move through fragments that share a layer. If you want the argument in longer form, go to Essay (the table of layers).

Technical sublime

The site treats surveying and geodesy as a kind of technical sublime: the promise that measurement can master distance, make the continent legible, and stabilize the world as geometry. But the mountain refuses permanence. Snow, ice, and neglect convert infrastructure into relic. What remains is not function, but trace.

Method

The fragments are written as montage: short blocks that can hold fieldnote, archival citation, material detail, and question side by side. Instead of a single linear narrative, the map offers an argument you can approach from multiple angles—ring by ring, layer by layer.
